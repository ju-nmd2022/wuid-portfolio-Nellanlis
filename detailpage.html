The IKEA Stool
As previously mentioned, I had painted the stool when I was younger and this is what it looked like before. it was originally just a wooden stool. This design that I did with a handmade cutout stencil of Mickey Mouse and some random circles had been standing in my room for quite some years and I felt it was time for a change.
I started off by painting the whole stool white, a few coats were required since the purple paint I previously used was quite strong. I also wanted to make sure none of the circles or Mickey Mouse was visible underneath the new one. This was also the time for me to decide on the new design I wanted to do.
After doing some brainstorming and googling, I found an inspiration picture online and started sketching my city. Buildings is not something I have drawn a lot of before so I was nervous before starting the sketch. Proportions is an important thing in a drawing like this.
The inspiration picture I looked at was in black and white but I knew that I wanted to include colors, neutral colors. With acrylic paints, I started mixing a lot of colors to get different nuances for the buildings. I for example only had two different tones of brown and in order to not make the image look flat, I mixed them with red, green, blue or yellow for variation.
Since the buildings and the city are concentrated at the bottom of the flat surface I felt that the top looked a bit empty. After some contemplation I decided to add some hot air balloons and clouds.
